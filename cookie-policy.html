Cookie Policy

Last Updated: January 2025

This Cookie Policy explains how OmniMind24 (operated by A.I. Help Pros LLP) uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website and use our services.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They help websites recognize your device and remember information about your visit, such as your preferences and login status.

Types of cookies we use:

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser
  • Persistent Cookies: Cookies that remain on your device until they expire or you delete them
  • First-Party Cookies: Cookies set by OmniMind24 directly
  • Third-Party Cookies: Cookies set by our service providers and partners

4. Local Storage and Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we use other browser storage technologies:

  • Local Storage: Stores larger amounts of data locally for performance and functionality

5. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

5.1 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit OmniMind24, you'll see a cookie consent banner allowing you to:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject non-essential cookies
  • Customize your cookie preferences

5.2 Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View and delete existing cookies
  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies only
  • Clear cookies when you close your browser
  • Receive notifications before cookies are set

Browser-specific instructions:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.3 Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of specific tracking services:

  • Google Analytics: Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Advertising: Digital Advertising Alliance Opt-Out
  • Network Advertising: NAI Opt-Out Tool

5.4 Do Not Track

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature. Currently, there is no industry standard for how to respond to DNT signals. We do not currently respond to DNT browser signals.

6. Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you disable or refuse cookies:

  • You may not be able to log in or use certain features
  • Some functionality may not work properly
  • Your preferences may not be remembered
  • Website performance may be degraded
  • You may see less relevant advertising

Essential cookies cannot be disabled without severely impacting website functionality and security.

7. Mobile Devices

Mobile devices also use tracking technologies similar to cookies:

  • Mobile Device IDs: Unique identifiers assigned to mobile devices
  • App Analytics: Usage tracking within mobile applications
  • Push Notifications: Tokens for delivering notifications

You can control mobile tracking through your device settings (iOS: Settings > Privacy; Android: Settings > Google > Ads).
